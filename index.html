What is Litecoin (LTC)?

Litecoin, often referred to as the "silver to Bitcoin's gold," is a decentralized digital currency founded by Charlie Lee in October 2011. It shares many similarities with Bitcoin but distinguishes itself through its faster block generation time and a different hashing algorithm called Scrypt.

With a maximum supply of 84 million coins, Litecoin offers a significant advantage in terms of scalability and transaction speed compared to other cryptocurrencies. What Blockchain Does Litecoin Use? Litecoin operates on its own blockchain, known as the Litecoin blockchain.
